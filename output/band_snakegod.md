### 19.02.2002 bios SNAKEGOD Band Info...

<p>SNAKEGOD was founded 1998 in Tampere, Finland. Right from the start, the northern Europeans were considered a secret top act. In '99 and 2000, the band recorded some demos, gathered its strength, and was able to raise the quality of their style continually; they established their own unique sound. <BR><BR> Already their first demo convinced the Finnish label Mastervox Records to sign the band, and shortly after, the German MAJOR MTM MUSIC & PUBLISHING showed big interest in the band, and purchased the licence for SNAKEGOD's debut CD "Invitation", as well as for the 3 following albums.<BR><BR> While recording their debut album "Invitation", two band members left and were replaced by Kari "Lacu" Lahtinen, who banged the drums for Michael Monroe (Hanoi Rocks), and Jani Hцlli on keyboards. <BR><BR> With "Invitation", SNAKEGOD, a new band with high expectations, gave a unique debut. Their music, definitely very Melodic Metal, is compared to classics like Whitesnake, Rainbow, and Deep Purple, with a touch of Dream Theatre, and Saga, mixed with their own style and the joy to play young Finns have. The album was mixed by the new HIM album producer TT Oksala. <BR><BR> "Invitation" was released worldwide on September 24th 2001.</p>

### 25.02.2002 articles Snakegod exclusive interview for Musica Must Die...

<p>with Jari Tiura (vocals), Marko Ruokonen (guitar), Pete Repo (bass), Jani Hцlli (keys)<BR><BR> 1) You play Melodic Metal. Can you described your own style? And why did you decide to play such kind of music - nu-metal, sympho-black and melodic death are top-sellers now? <BR><BR> Marko: Our style is difficult to describe because there are so many different types of songs on our album. Our influences come basically from Heavy Rock. We try to find the own story of each of our songs, thatswhy it's perhaps a bit complicated to locate an overall impression. Many elements come from the melodic Heavy/ Hard Rock of the 80ies and 90ies. In those days the music was very pure, the melodies and the spirit. Nowadays you mostly find only two kinds of moods: sadness or anger. Nu-metal is quite defiant and doesn't fit to us. Black Metal and Death Metal have again too much attitude. And we are more the romantic types. When we made our debut album, we actually didn't even think about the commercial aspect. Our music is created somehow naturally, so we don't really need to sit down and think much about it.<BR> Pete: So you could say, our style is a mix of modern Metal and classical Hard Rock, and there are also ingredients of different Metal styles. We all like old Hard Rock very much, so we simply wanted to play and compose that kind of music. We know that our style is maybe not so commercial nowadays but there are still many people which like the older stuff. <BR> Jari: Yes, so maybe destiny chose our style. We just like to continue the old master's work but with a modern touch.<BR><BR> 2) Your influences and roots? <BR><BR> Pete: Our influences are Whitesnake, Rainbow, Deep Purple, Dio, Yngwie J. Malmsteen, Uriah Heep, Black Sabbath and other great Hard Rock bands. <BR> Jari: My personal influences are of course also singers like Dio, Coverdale, Byron, Jeff Scott Soto, Graham Bonnet <BR><BR> 3) Who the hell is a Snakegod? :-))) <BR><BR> Pete: The Snakegod is a god in Egyptian mythology. <BR><BR> 4) Do you have music experience before? (Bands, projects e.t.c.) <BR><BR> Jari: Yes, I've been with a few Metal bands, like Burbon and Crommer.<BR> Marko: All kinds of musical areas and bands, from Rock to Blues to Heavy.<BR> Pete: Same here, lots of bands and projects but this is the first one with a record deal.<BR> Jani: Yes, I've also been with many different kinds of bands, cover bands and also several famous Finnish artists. But Snakegod is also my first band with a record deal.<BR><BR> 5) What do you think about your collegues from Finland? Finntroll, Stratovarius, Kalmah, Children Of Bodom? <BR><BR> Jari: I think they are high quality bands in their own styles. My personal favourite would be Tarot though. Pete: Stratovarius and COB are great bands, and they have well succeeded. I haven't heard Finntroll and Kalmah yet, I only know their names. <BR> Jani: My favourite of these is Stratovarius, and also Tarot is very good. Besides these I would mention Sonata Arctica from Finland, which got big popularity in a short time.<BR> Marko: I think it is great that lately so many top bands come from Finland. Specially many great Heavy bands. It seems that Heavy music fits good to the Finnish soul and nature, as there are also many Heavy fans in Finland. You feel appreciated abroad, when you say you come from Finland, the Stratos and Bodoms have opened a door for many other bands. And of course also the production - they know how to produce and mix Heavy music in Finland. A band or a song can be ever so good, but a bad production can really ruin it.<BR><BR> 6) Do you like death-metal?<BR><BR> Pete: Nope, it's too dark and aggressive.<BR> Jari: No, I don't.<BR> Marko: It fits sometimes for a special mood, but I don't listen to it much.<BR> Jani: I don't like it!<BR><BR> 7) Do you know something about Russian (CIS) or Soviet metal bands? <BR><BR> Jari: I'm afraid I don't.<BR> Pete: There was a band called Gorky Park which was quite good but that's all I know about the Soviet Metal scene. <BR> Jani: Yes, Gorky Park is maybe the most famous Russian/Soviet rock band. I think that GP got quite much attention all over the world.<BR> Marko: I have heard a few, but not many. The marketing should really get better.<BR><BR> 8) Do you know russian classic composers (Stravinskiy, Musorgskiy, Tchaykovskiy e.t.c.)? <BR><BR> Jani: Yes, of course. I started my classical piano lessons at the age of nine, and have played many Tchaykovskiy compositions. The most famous operas and ballets are "Swan lake" and "Nut cracker".<BR> Pete: I know all those names of course. And the "Nutcracker" is famous.<BR> Jari: Yes, same here. <BR> Marko: Well, the world's best composers came from Russia (well, we do have Sibelius). It seems the situation for classical music is really good in Russia. Many people are interested in it and highly appreciate it. Unfortunately here in Finland, the classical music is badly forgotten amongst the younger folks.<BR><BR> 9) Your future plans... <BR><BR> Marko: Lets fight and get Karjala back, and then the rest of the world (joke).<BR> Jani: I'd say the most important issue right now is touring, and then to do a killing second album!<BR> Pete: Well, there are of course many plans going on right now, regarding touring and also the next album for example, but it's a little too early to talk about it. The best is, to check our website www.snakegod.com, because it gets updated regularly with the latest news.<BR></p>

### 25.02.2002 articles Змеебог. Эксклюзивное интервью для Musica Must Die....

<p>Jari Tiura (вокал), Marko Ruokonen (гитара), Pete Repo (басс), Jani Holli (клавиши)<BR><BR> 1) Ребята, вы играете Мелодичный Метал. Как вы могли бы сами описать собственный стиль? И почему именно такая музыка, а не nu-metal, sympho-black или melodic death - то что сейчас хорошо продается? <BR><BR> Marko: Наш стиль трудно описать словами - слишком уж много разновидностей песен на этом альбоме. Вдохновляет нас в основном Тяжелый Рок. Мы постарались создать нечто особенное для каждой вещи, вот почему трудно рассказать об этом вкратце. Многие элементы пришли к нам из мелодичного хэви/хард-рока 80-х и 90-х. В те дни музыка была очень простой, но мелодичной и душевной. В наши дни в основном можно найти или печаль или агрессию. Nu-metal - слишком вызывающ для нас. Black Metal и Death Metal слишком много внимания уделяют свой идейной позиции. Мы более романтичные натуры. Когда мы делали первый альбом, мы даже не задумывались о коммерческом аспекте. Наша музыка - это нечто натуральное, поэтому нам не нужно сидеть и задумываться слишком много о ней. <BR> Pete: Как вы можете слышать, наш стиль это микс между современным металлом и классическим хард-роком, также в нем присутствуют и элементы других стилей. Все мы любим хард-рок старой школы и в общем то мы и хотим играть музыку такого плана. Сейчас, наверное, наш стиль не очень популярен, но все же довольно много людей любят подобное "старье".<BR> Jari: Возможно провидение избрало за нас наш стиль. Мы хотели бы просто продолжить работу старых мастеров, но в современном ключе.<BR><BR> 2) Ваши корни и кумиры? <BR><BR> Pete: Наши кумиры это: Whitesnake, Rainbow, Deep Purple, Dio, Yngwie J. Malmsteen, Uriah Heep, Black Sabbath и другие хард рок команды. <BR> Jari: Мои персональные корни это Dio, Coverdale, Byron, Jeff Scott Soto, Graham Bonnet. <BR><BR> 3) Что за "хрень" такая Змеебог? :-)))<BR><BR> Pete: Это бог в египетской мифологии.<BR><BR> 4) Был ли у вас музыкальный опыт до Змеебога? <BR><BR> Jari: Да я участвовал в командах Burbon и Crommer.<BR> Marko: Разные стили от блюза до хэви.<BR> Pete: Да, много команд и проектов, но это первый с контрактом на запись.<BR> Jani: Много в т.ч. и известных финнских команд, но Snakegod - первая с контрактом. <BR><BR> 5) Что вы думаете о ваших коллегах из Финляндии? Finntroll, Stratovarius, Kalmah, Children Of Bodom? <BR><BR> Jari: Это команды высочайшего класса в своих стилях. Мой личный фаворит - Tarot. <BR> Pete: Stratovarius и COB - великие команды достигшие большого успеха. Я пока не слышал Finntroll и Kalmah, знаю только по названиям. <BR> Jani: Мои любимцы - Stratovarius, Tarot - также очень хорош. Еще хотелось бы отметить Sonata Arctica, которые приобрели большую популярность за короткое время. <BR> Marko: Это прекрасно, что в последнее время появилось очень много топ-команд из Финляндии. Особенно прекрасны хэви-команды. Похоже что тяжелая музыка хорошо подходит финнской натуре и душе. Сейчас Страты и Бодомы открыли дорогу многим молодым командам. И продюссирование. Сейчас финны знают как продюссировать и микшировать тяжелую музыку. Команда может быть великолепна, но плохое продюссирование может убить ее.<BR><BR> 6) Нравиться ли вам death-metal?<BR><BR> Pete: Нет. Он слишком мрачный и агррессивный.<BR> Jari: Мне нет.<BR> Marko: Это на любителя. Я много не могу слушать.<BR> Jani: Мне не нравится!<BR><BR> 7) Знаете ли вы что нибудь о советских или СНГовских металловых командах? <BR><BR> Jari: Боюсь, что нет.<BR> Pete: Была такая команда "Парк Горького", классная кстати.
